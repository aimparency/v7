# NEAR: Best New or Continued Project

## Challenge Information
- **Sponsor:** NEAR Protocol
- **Prize Pool:** $500
- **Category:** Continued Development

## Challenge Overview

This challenge rewards the best new or continued projects building on NEAR, with a focus on teams that demonstrate clear technical progress, strong execution, and momentum beyond the NEAR Innovation Sandbox hackathon. Projects that previously participated in the NEAR Innovation Sandbox hackathon are eligible and encouraged to continue building, iterate meaningfully, and level up their work within PL_Genesis: Frontiers of Collaboration.

We are looking for:
- Net-new projects built during PL_Genesis, or
- Continued projects originating from the NEAR Innovation Sandbox hackathon that demonstrate clear improvements, expansion, or refinement

## Challenge Details

Build AI products that people keep using because they save time, reduce risk, or unlock a workflow that was previously not safe or practical.

### Good fits for this track:

- **Autonomous systems** that keep working after the user closes the tab. Think "already working on the thing you want", with a clear boundary for what the system can and cannot do.
- **User-owned memory and knowledge** that is portable, inspectable, and revocable. If your product depends on memory, treat it as user data, not app data.
- **Payments as part of the workflow**, not a checkout screen. If an agent can request, route, or schedule payments, the payment logic should be auditable and constrained.

We're not looking for chatbot demos. We're looking for products with a real user, a real job-to-be-done, and a path to continued use.

"One job" is encouraged: a single, bounded workflow the agent can do end-to-end (but broader systems are welcome if the boundaries and controls are still clear).

## Submission Requirements

- summary
- github
- demo

## Judging Criteria

### Technical Execution
The complexity, robustness, and quality of the code or architecture — does it work and was it hard to build?

### User Experience (UX)
Is the product intuitive, accessible, and visually polished? Would a user understand how to use it quickly?

### Impact/Usefulness
Does the project solve a real problem? Could it meaningfully improve lives or systems if deployed?

### Completeness/Functionality
Is the product finished and fully functional? Or is it just a prototype with broken elements?
