# Ten-Slide Deck Outline

## 1. Aimparency

Git-native intent and dependency infrastructure for AI-assisted development.

Ask: EUR 15,000 minimum bridge; EUR 50,000 target.

## 2. The Problem

Agents accelerate implementation while fragmenting intent across prompts,
chats, tickets, and repositories. Teams lose the durable chain from purpose to
dependency to verified result.

## 3. The Product

Show the live aim graph beside a repository. One sentence: humans edit intent;
agents read and update the same durable model.

## 4. Product Proof

Show:

- `.bowman/` in Git;
- prioritized aims;
- Codex or another agent reading aim context;
- a completed change linked back to its aim;
- human/external waiting states and experiment evidence.

## 5. Why Existing Tools Are Insufficient

Compare tickets, chat history, Git, and Aimparency across durable intent,
dependency graph, agent readability, local ownership, and verification.

## 6. Beachhead

Technical founders, open-source maintainers, and AI-native teams operating
multiple agents or long-running repositories.

Validation method: 20 interviews and three paid design partnerships.

## 7. Business Model

Open-source local core. Potential paid layers: hosted collaboration, managed
agent execution, organizational controls, and direct support. Validate before
fixing packaging.

## 8. Six-Month Milestones

Three paid partners; reliable provenance; measurable loop experiments;
published open-source workflow; evidence-based financing decision.

## 9. Team

Felix: technical founder, system architecture, product.

Robin: business/operations, Mindance exit, MBA WHU. Confirm participation before
publishing.

## 10. Ask

EUR 15,000 minimum by 31 August 2026. EUR 50,000 target bridge. Show budget,
milestones, and the specific introductions sought.
