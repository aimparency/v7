# Wilde Moehre Planning Pilot

## Internal Proposal

Try Aimparency on one real Wilde Moehre planning workflow at no cost. Felix
sets up and maintains the model; the participating team only needs to validate
whether it reflects reality and use it during normal planning.

The purpose is not to replace current planning software. Aimparency adds an
explicit map of goals, dependencies, unresolved decisions, and blocked work
across the tools already in use.

## Choose One Workflow

Pick the workflow with the clearest owner, real dependencies, and decisions
still ahead:

1. **Site readiness:** permits, infrastructure, suppliers, build order, and
   readiness dependencies.
2. **Program delivery:** bookings, technical requirements, scheduling, and
   hand-offs.
3. **Crew readiness:** roles, staffing gaps, onboarding, shifts, and
   prerequisites.

Do not model the whole festival in the first pilot.

## Four-Week Scope

### Week 1: Map

- Select one workflow and one accountable contact.
- Import or manually capture 20-50 current aims and dependencies.
- Mark owners, status, human-dependent actions, and unresolved decisions.
- Review the first map for factual errors.

### Weeks 2-3: Use

- Update the map during existing planning work.
- Record newly discovered dependencies and blockers.
- Use one short weekly review to identify the next valuable actions.
- Keep accounting, messaging, ticketing, and operational records in their
  existing systems.

### Week 4: Evaluate

- Compare the final map with the initial state.
- Interview 3-5 participants for concrete benefits and friction.
- Decide whether another workflow is worth modelling.
- Request permission before using names, screenshots, or quotes externally.

## Success Evidence

Capture:

- dependencies or blockers discovered earlier than they otherwise would be;
- duplicated or unnecessary work avoided;
- decisions clarified, including who had to make them;
- planning questions answered faster;
- number of people who returned to or updated the model;
- willingness to continue, recommend, or pay for a supported version.

Avoid claiming time or money saved without a defensible baseline.

## Initial Ask

> I am building an open-source tool that maps goals and dependencies across
> complex work. I would like to model one bounded Wilde Moehre planning
> workflow for free and maintain it for four weeks. It will not replace any
> existing tool. I need one contact who can validate the model and a short
> weekly review. At the end we decide from evidence whether it was useful.

## Decisions Needed From Wilde Moehre

- Which workflow should be tested?
- Who owns it and can validate the model?
- What information may be entered?
- Where must sensitive information remain abstract or excluded?
- Who may see the model?
- May anonymized findings be used in a case study?

## Stop Conditions

Stop or narrow the pilot if:

- no accountable workflow owner participates;
- maintaining the map creates more work than it resolves;
- sensitive data cannot be represented safely;
- the model duplicates an existing source of truth without adding dependency
  or decision visibility.

## Commercial Follow-Up

The pilot is free. A follow-up is a separate decision and could cover another
workflow, onboarding, direct support, integrations, or a documented planning
method. Do not imply a purchase obligation during the pilot.
