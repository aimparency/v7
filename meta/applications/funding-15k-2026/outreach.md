# Funding and Design-Partner Outreach

## Warm Angel Introduction

Subject: Introduction: Git-native intent infrastructure for coding agents

I am building Aimparency, a local-first intent and dependency graph for
AI-assisted development. Git preserves what agents changed; Aimparency
preserves why the work exists, how goals depend on each other, and what evidence
shows completion.

The prototype is working with Codex, Claude, Gemini, MCP, semantic search, and a
resumable autonomous loop. I am raising a small bridge: EUR 15,000 minimum by
31 August, toward a EUR 50,000 target, to fund six months of customer validation
and three paid design partnerships.

Would you be open to a 20-minute product demonstration, or introduce me to an
angel who invests in developer infrastructure or open-source AI?

## Design-Partner Message

Subject: Paid design partnership for multi-agent development

I am looking for three teams whose coding-agent work has outgrown prompts and
flat ticket queues.

Aimparency keeps goals, dependencies, priorities, evidence, and agent context in
Git beside the code. The proposed design partnership is EUR 5,000 for six to
eight weeks: onboarding, direct founder support, roadmap influence, and a
documented workflow on one real repository.

This is not outsourced feature development. The purpose is to determine whether
a durable intent layer materially improves your team's agent coordination.

Could we spend 20 minutes checking whether the problem is real for your team?

## Qualification Questions

1. How many coding agents or agent sessions does the team use each week?
2. Where is the reason behind agent-generated changes currently recorded?
3. What breaks when work spans several sessions, repositories, or people?
4. Who can revise agent priorities and authorization boundaries?
5. How does the team decide that an agent's result actually fulfilled intent?
6. Has coordination loss caused rework, delay, or an unsafe change recently?
7. Would solving this be worth EUR 5,000 over eight weeks? Why or why not?
