# Neurotech

## Challenge Information
- **Sponsor:** Protocol Labs
- **Prize Pool:** $6,000
- **Category:** Protocol Labs Track

## Challenge Overview

Explore systems at the intersection of cognition, coordination, and computation.

This track spans BCI, neuro-inspired architectures, collective intelligence tooling, and speculative neurotech infrastructure. As brain-computer interfaces move from lab to market and AI systems increasingly model cognitive processes, questions of neural data rights, cognitive sovereignty, and safe augmentation become urgent design problems.

Projects may be experimental or speculative, but must demonstrate conceptual rigor with consideration on safety and ethics.

## Eligibility

Projects must qualify as submissions to sponsor challenges to compete for track prizes.

## Prizes

- **1st Place:** $3,000
- **2nd Place:** $2,000
- **3rd Place:** $1,000

## Example Project Ideas

### Brain-Computer Interfaces
- Neural data wallets with granular consent and revocation controls
- Open-source BCI signal processing pipelines for accessibility devices
- Federated learning systems that train on neural data without centralized collection

### Collective Intelligence
- Deliberation platforms that synthesize group reasoning into coherent outputs
- Swarm decision tools for real-time distributed consensus
- Annotation systems where crowds collaboratively train AI models

### Neuro-Inspired Systems
- Spiking neural network implementations for energy-efficient edge computing
- Memory architectures modeled on hippocampal consolidation
- Attention mechanisms informed by cognitive load research

### Safety & Ethics
- Cognitive liberty frameworks with enforceable boundaries
- Adversarial testing for BCI systems against manipulation
- Consent protocols for neurofeedback and augmentation tools

### Consumer
- Focus and flow-state apps using non-invasive EEG feedback
- Meditation tools with real-time brainwave visualization
- Collaborative thinking environments that map group attention and engagement
