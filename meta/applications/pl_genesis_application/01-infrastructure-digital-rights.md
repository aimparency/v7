# Infrastructure & Digital Rights

## Challenge Information
- **Sponsor:** Protocol Labs
- **Prize Pool:** $6,000
- **Category:** Protocol Labs Track

## Challenge Overview

Build the foundational systems that secure the internet and expand digital human rights.

This track centres on decentralised cloud networks, data ownership, privacy-preserving technologies, cryptography, and censorship-resistant communication—the building blocks of an open, resilient, user-owned internet.

## Eligibility

Projects must qualify as submissions to sponsor challenges to compete for track prizes.

## Prizes

- **1st Place:** $3,000
- **2nd Place:** $2,000
- **3rd Place:** $1,000

## Example Project Ideas

### Decentralized Infrastructure
- P2P VPNs where nodes earn for bandwidth contribution
- Distributed CDNs for media delivery without central chokepoints

### Data Ownership
- Personal data vaults with granular consent and monetization controls
- Portable social graphs users can take across apps

### Consumer
- Password managers with social recovery and no central server
- File storage apps with client-side encryption and token incentives
- "Dead man's switch" for digital inheritance and secure key transfer

## Submission Requirements

- summary
- video
- demo

## Judging Criteria

### Innovation/Creativity
How original, novel, or imaginative the idea is — does it introduce something new or approach an old problem in a new way?

### Impact/Usefulness
Does the project solve a real problem? Could it meaningfully improve lives or systems if deployed?

### Relevance to Theme/Challenge
How well does the submission align with the specific track, sponsor challenge, or hackathon theme?

### Use of Sponsor Tech/APIs
Especially in bounty-driven hackathons — how well did the team integrate and showcase the required sponsor technology?
